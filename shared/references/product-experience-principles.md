# Product Experience Principles

Use this as the owner-approved product/design lens for UX, admin, onboarding, settings, defaults, and product polish decisions.

## Product Experience Goal

Build premium WordPress products that feel enterprise-grade, simple by default, and powerful when needed. Non-technical users should get value quickly with minimal setup, while developers and advanced users should have clean, well-organized controls for customization, automation, and deeper integration.

Every product should feel polished, trustworthy, and enterprise-ready. The default path should be plug-and-play for non-technical users: clear onboarding, smart defaults, minimal required settings, and fast time-to-value. Advanced configuration should exist for developers and power users, but stay organized, contextual, and out of the way of the basic workflow.

## Product Implications

- Prefer strong defaults over more settings.
- Do not add settings unless they unlock real value, safety, or developer flexibility.
- Keep onboarding clear, required setup minimal, and time-to-value fast.
- Keep advanced controls organized, contextual, and out of the way of the default workflow.
- Treat onboarding, settings, docs UX, and premium polish as product-quality work, not afterthoughts.

## Review Lens

For UX/admin/onboarding/settings work, check:

- Can a non-technical user get value quickly with minimal setup?
- Are defaults smart enough that the product feels mostly plug-and-play?
- Are advanced controls present for developers and power users without blocking the basic workflow?
- Does the UI feel polished, trustworthy, and enterprise-ready?
